# PALIMPSEST: Lanterns of the Drowned Archive

## Pitch

3D action-exploration roguelite for players who like readable skill combat, discovery, melancholy mystery, and choices without obvious moral labels. The world is an archive built to preserve a drowned civilization. Its memories became self-aware; keeping every past alive is now killing the present.

Player is the last Lamplighter. Enter unstable memory islands, fight Hollows, recover Echoes, then Preserve, Edit, or Release pivotal memories. Each choice changes future run rules, narration, unlocks, and the final truth.

## Pillars

- **Flow:** responsive movement, dash, aimed pulse, resonance field; clean silhouettes and generous tells.
- **Meaning:** enemies and upgrades express the theme: memory can shelter, distort, or imprison.
- **Discovery:** short environmental stories + Archive journal assemble into multiple valid interpretations.
- **Return:** procedural island layouts, escalating weekly-style “Tides,” build choices, three story alignments/endings.
- **Respect:** no grind walls, no dark patterns, adjustable difficulty, remappable controls, readable UI.

## Release scope

- 20–35 minute complete campaign loop; 2+ hours to see all conclusions.
- Repeatable expeditions with seeded layouts, three enemy families, elites, boss, 18 boons, 24 lore records.
- Meta Archive with persistent unlocks and choice history.
- Keyboard/mouse + controller, settings, pause, onboarding, save migration, credits.

## Visual/audio identity

Deep ink-blue void, oxidized brass architecture, warm amber player light, cyan preserved memories, magenta corrupted edits. Stylized low-poly geometry with strong fog layers and emissive guidance. Music is restrained glass/choir ambience; combat adds pulse percussion. Never photoreal, never visual noise.

## Lore spine

The Archive was not built after the Flood—it caused it. The city outsourced grief to a machine called the Curator. Denied memories accumulated physical pressure until the sea rose from beneath the streets. The Curator split itself into Witness, Mercy, and Continuance. Each asks the player to privilege a different kind of truth. No ending is labeled good.
