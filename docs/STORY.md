# "Peacebound" Game Story

This game is about a cruel and dark fantasy world.


## The game history

(this is spoiler for players, players shouldn't be aware of this at first).
300 years ago, a great war happens between the 4 Great Kings, to capture a powerful crystal known as "The World Crystal". Whoever obtains that crystal, can force some rules on the world and no one else can ever EVER disobey that rule.
The 4 Great Kings were:
  - The Mage King (race: human)
  - The Sword King (race: human)
  - The Ogre King (race: Ogre)
  - The Demon King (race: Demon)

After many wars, sacrifices, tricks and huge conflicts, The Mage King won. He obtained "The World Crystal".
The Mage King was a kind human, all he wanted to do was to end all the conflicts in the world; that was his only wish after sacficing so many humans, ogres and demons. He was seeing all races as equal, yet he had to go against his morals and kill so many of them, just to prevent such a huge power from reaching the hands of the corrupted Kings.

The Mage King reached to the Chamber that had The World Crystal hidden in it.
He couldn't wait to wish from the Crystal: "I want the entire world to fall in absolute peace, no more bloods"

He saw The World Crystal in front of him, shining, like a Holy Object, coming from a Deity.

He got closer to it, and touched the crystal.
The moment he touched it, a massive amount of Mana flowed into his mind.

The World Crystal started talking to him:
  - "I ask tho, what is thy wish?"
  + ....I...I want absolute peace in the world
  - "hoooo".... said the crystal while whispering in his head
  - "If thy wish absolute peace, can thy protect this wish to the end?"
  + "what? what do you mean?"
  - "What if tomorrow, you die due to old age? Then thy wish will become obsolete"
  + "what...should I do then?"
  - "ha ha ha... well, that's easy, first of all, wish for your immortality, so long as you live, you can keep the peace"
  + "But how can you grant immortality? You are a World Crystal...."
  - "That's easy...."

And so, The World Crystal suggested a system to be applied to the entire world:
The system rule goes like this:
  1. To be able to advance to next level, you need ((current_level + 1) * 4) XP points.
  2. To be able to gain XP points, you need to kill alive beings (tree, plants and other things that are considered as "alive" but cannot defend themselves / resist won't count (such as fish, insects, normal sheeps, etc)).
  3. If you kill creatures that are not of the same race as you, no matter what their level, or your level is, you will always ever gain 1 XP for each kill.
  4. If you kill an alive being of your same race, you can gain an XP equal to their (current_level * 4). However, their own level will go down by 4 (regardless of their race) and their current_xp point will be reset to 0. Right, that means, if an alive being is less than level 5, they will permanently die upon being killed for the first time. If an alive being is higher than (or equal to) level 5, e.g. level 10, their level will be decreased by 4 (e.g. to 6,) but they will also be "reborn" (in the same age, before getting any injuries) in their "home place"; BUT, with one important rule: their HP will be equal to 10 upon being reborn.
  5. Every 20 years that an alive being ages, their level will automatically decrease by 4 (this doesn't happen gradually, it's in the game lore that the "exact moment" the level decrease happens); but this rule will also make **all** humans immortal to dying due to age: for example if you can only reach level 20, you can live up to 100 years old. But if you can't reach level 5 by the time your age is turned to 20, your level becomes 0 and you will **always** die permanently and instantly if your level is turned to 0.
  6. The Mage King is turned level 60 the moment these rules are applied by the World Crystal; meaning he can live for the next 300 years without dying from age, but also he has to hunt other high-level humans from time to time to be able to keep living. All other alive beings are turned level 1 instantly (and everyone who is born, will be born with level 1). Those people who are already at age of 20+, would need to get to level 5 in the next 20 years (so it works by "time difference", not just absolute age).
  7. All the alive beings in the world are forbidden from having Weapons. They can only ever use Magic (and perhaps their...fist).

extra notes:
  - xp overflow will carry to next level.
  - "The reborn place" is at first the place of birth. The Player can later on buy "Special Reborn-Crystal". When they break that crystal in a place, that place gets registered as their new "Reborn Location".
  - All physical tools that can harm others are included in the weapon ban. E.g. a kitchen knife is included. Which is why humanity started making all kind of magical spell.
  - The aging rule applies to ALL alive beings. Ogres, Demons, Humans, etc. Small animals such as insects, fishes, etc are all excluded from the system. However, a Spider Boss is not excluded. Anyway the game is not really going to count "20 years" for insects and spiders, this is just the lore of the game.
  - Every animal in the game that are not "aggressive" will not give you any xp. E.g. once you attack a sheep, it will just run away. But if you attack a wolf, it will fight back, so the kill must be "meaningful". (if you can one-shot a wolf with a trap it will also work).

The last rule tempted The Mage King. "I'm the only Mage King in the world, I know Magecraft more than anyone else....so I can keep being at the top forever, and keep the peace in the world"
He also thought to himself: "well, people can just keep killing wild beast animals to gain just enough XP to keep living forever... Any race in the world would need at the very least only 56 XP per 20 years...just kill 3 wild animals like wolves per year and you can live forever! this is such a wonderful system!"
He then called these rules "The Peace Pact", and thought to himself "Since I will be the strongest in the world, I will make a central government and punish anyone who does murder"

And so, The Mage King agreed to these rules, thinking they are a blessing to the world, and since every human can obtain immortality easily, everyone will stop fighting and killing everyone (his brain was also being corrupted by The World Crystal with huge amount of Mana flowing into his brain).

And so the New World Rules went in effects in that day. And everyone were forced to either learn Magic...or die.


## Lore

(the game story that players see starts from here):

The story happens ~300 years after "The day that World Rules Changed". The Player is living in a peaceful village, with his lovely family. His brother just went to the capital city few days ago to find new jobs. He has a sister of age 25 and a mother and a dad.
The Player is turned 21 years, and he has level 13.
He's inside of the house that.... he hears sounds of horses, a lot of them. And after some time, sounds of screams and fire. The Player and his family get out of their house and see....the village is being raided, burned and their villagers are being murdered one by one. And they don't just kill and pass, they wait until the villagers get reborn in their house and they kill them again, until their level reaches 0 and die.

The player and his family tries to resist by using their magic, but they can't, because the raiders level is higher than them and they know more powerful magic than them.

As a result, The Player sees his family getting slaughtered in front of him, multiple times. (their family members were around level ~20).

The Player himself dies 3 times, but since he was born in a deep chamber under the ground, the last time that he gets "reborn", he just pass out due to exhaustion and survives the raid.

The moment he wakes up (he's at level 1 then, this is where the player starts truly controling the character), he comes out of their house's underground chamber and sees the entire village is destroyed and burned to hell.

He then decides to go and kill/farm animals to level up. He's very depressed, but then he decides to go to the Capital city, where his brother is, to meet him and tell him what happened. He also believes he can talk about the group of raiders to the capital authorities to get them punished.

(spoiler: the village was actually deep inside of the forest, so no one really knew where were the routes, etc. The Player's brother was the one told the raiders' group the exact location, and The Player is about to find that out later. Tho the brother also did have its own reasons, which will be revealed later).
