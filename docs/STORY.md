# LUMENFALL Story Canon

## Premise

LUMENFALL: The First Crossing begins when Nia, a young rift mechanic in the frontier village of Hearthmere, tries to summon a lantern and summons the Wayfarer instead. Their arrival wakes the village's sleeping Waystone. The sky answers, and a rift opens.

Rifts are not natural disasters. They are wounds left by roads, names, and lives deliberately removed from the world's memory. Memory condenses around those wounds as singing riftglass. Some creatures called guardians are people whose names were erased. Repair therefore means more than closing portals: it means deciding who is remembered, who may cross, and who pays for safety.

## People

- **The Wayfarer:** player character, accidental arrival, and outsider able to hear what the world was forced to forget.
- **Nia:** rift mechanic responsible for the summoning. Curious, practical, and unwilling to treat people as tools. She braids the Memory Compass and pays honest wages rather than framing violence as loot.
- **Bram:** craftsperson. Builds the Lantern Lens at his physical workshop bench. Believes a tool should leave a place better than it found it and reveal only truths its bearer can survive.
- **Mara:** Hearthmere's field leader and guardian. Cautious rather than cowardly; understands rift behavior and guides the first expedition beyond the lantern road.
- **Pip:** a village child whose wonder and blunt observations keep the mystery human.
- **Aster:** the first rift hound whose memory is recovered: small hands, a red ribbon, and a promise to return before the bells. Aster was fleeing something that knew their name, not hunting Hearthmere.
- **Iven:** a missing villager trapped in a looping apology, always walking north and always returning.
- **Sola:** a missing villager shown countless possible homes by the Amberfen Orrery, none containing her daughter.
- **Orin:** a missing villager whose name was nearly erased by Bellscar's silent bell.
- **The Forgotten Architect:** an echo glimpsed on the Witness path. They ask the Wayfarer to find the Glass Road before “they erase me twice.”

## Implemented chapters

### I. The Wrong Star

The Wayfarer appears in Hearthmere in place of Nia's intended lantern. Nia asks for help waking the dormant Waystone. When it awakens, the sky responds and exposes the first rift.

### II. Singing Shards

Mara sends the Wayfarer to collect three singing pieces of riftglass. Bram identifies them as ownerless memories and crafts a Lantern Lens—a tool for seeing truths hidden by fear and erasure.

### III. Aster and the Eastern Road

Mara opens the eastern lantern road. A frightened rift hound attacks, but the Lens reveals the person inside it: Aster. The recovered memory proves that rifts remember names and that a “monster” may be a victim still running from its pursuer. Nia pays eighteen hearth-coins as wages for dangerous work.

### IV. The Three Instruments

Mara's field map offers three investigations in any order:

- The **Glasswood Shrine** repeats the truth that every guardian was once a human name.
- The **Amberfen Orrery** charts the rift as the shadow of a road removed from memory.
- The **Bellscar Bell** remembers being rung by someone not yet born.

All three instruments point to the same blank road. Nia pays forty hearth-coins and concludes that the instruments must be opened together—or not at all.

### V. Names in the Wind

The Wayfarer rescues Iven, Sola, and Orin from three different memory prisons. Each gives a memory thread. Nia pays thirty-six hearth-coins; at Bram's bench, the three threads become a Memory Compass. Unlike an ordinary compass, it points toward what the world was forced to forget.

### VI. Crossing of Three Voices

The Compass opens the Echo Causeway, a floating road of memory. Three altars wake through the rescued voices:

- Iven: a road is every apology made while walking home.
- Sola: choosing one home freely is not betrayal of all the homes refused.
- Orin: a name survives when another person answers it.

At the central gate, the Causeway asks what a road may become. The player makes a persistent choice with no simple good answer:

- **Shelter:** fold the road closed for safety. The forgotten remain unreachable, and something begins testing the sealed edge. The Waystone forms a sheltering crystal ring.
- **Bridge:** bind the road to Iven, Sola, and Orin's living names. Passage remains possible, but its anchors bear an ongoing cost and will need rules. The Waystone forms three linked rings.
- **Witness:** cross without owning or closing the road. The Wayfarer sees the person who erased it and hears the Forgotten Architect's warning. A ghostly echo remains at the Waystone.

### VII. The Glass Road

The Wayfarer reports the choice to Nia and receives seventy-five hearth-coins. The changed Waystone confirms the central revelation: rifts were made, not born. Its new light points west toward the Glass Road.

The present playable story hands off at the objective to follow that light west. Choice-specific evidence remains: Shelter's edge is being tested, Bridge breathes through Orin's name, and Witness carries the Architect's plea.

## Themes

- Identity is relational: a name survives because someone answers it.
- Safety, access, and truth impose different costs; none is automatically virtuous.
- A chosen home does not betray every life or home left unchosen.
- Repair must respect people, consent, labor, and consequence.
- Fear can conceal truth. An apparent enemy may be a person trapped in another shape.
- Erasure is intentional and political, not merely magical weather.
- Roads represent relationship, migration, apology, ownership, and shared memory.

The three Causeway choices must create lasting consequences and relationships without deleting exploration or invalidating the player's world.

## Unresolved canon and planned arc

These questions are established but not yet answered in playable content:

- Who removed the road, and why?
- Who are “they,” and how could they erase the Forgotten Architect twice?
- What is the Glass Road, and who still travels it?
- Why does Bellscar remember a future ringing?
- How were named people transformed into guardians?
- What happened on Nia's failed expedition, and what is the Borrowed Sky?

Future acts may develop the Glass Road, the Borrowed Sky, Hearthmere's relationships, and the long consequences of the First Crossing. Treat those as planned direction, not implemented fact. New lore must extend this canon rather than silently contradicting it.
